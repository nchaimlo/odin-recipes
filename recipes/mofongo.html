Mofongo

Description

Mofongo is a beloved Puerto Rican dish made from mashed plantains. The dish starts with green plantains that are fried until crispy and then mashed together with garlic, olive oil, and pork cracklings (chicharr√≥n) or bacon. The result is a rich, savory mash with a delightful texture that's both crispy and tender. Mofongo can be served as a side dish or as a main course, often accompanied by a variety of savory options such as seafood, chicken, or stewed meats. Its deep, flavorful taste and versatile nature make it a staple in Puerto Rican cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 4 green plantains
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup pork cracklings (or perferred pork substitute)
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Peel plaintains and cut them into 1 inch slices.
  2. Heat a generous amount of oil in a frying pan over medium heat.
  3. Fry the plantain slices in batches until they are cooked through, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  4. Place plaintains in a large mixing bowl.
  5. Add minced garlic, olive oil, salt, pepper, and chopped pork cracklings and mash together. The mixture should be chunky but well-combined.
  6. Mold the mofongo into balls of desired size and serve hot.
