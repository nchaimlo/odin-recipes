Japchae

Description

Japchae is a delightful Korean noodle dish known for its slightly sweet and savory flavor. Made with potato starch noodles, this dish is highly versatile and allows for a variety of ingredients to be included or omitted based on personal preference. For instance, if mushrooms aren't to your taste, simply leave them out without compromising the overall flavor. In my opinion, Japchae is an excellent way to enjoy a medley of vegetables, making it both nutritious and customizable.

This recipe comes from Maangchi, a renowned Korean YouTuber celebrated for her authentic traditional Korean recipes. While I’m often cautious about online recipes and whether they will meet my expectations, I can confidently say that Maangchi’s recipe is sure to exceed them.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 3 tbsp sugar (brown or white)
  • 1/2 to 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • 5 garlic cloves, minced
  • 8 oz sweet potato starch noodles (dangmyeon): soak in water for 40 min
  • 1 large carrot
  • 1 zucchini
  • 8 oz spinach
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 tbsp + 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds
  • 1 tbsp toasted sesame oil

Steps

  1. Combine soy sauce, sugar, ground black pepper, and garlic in a bowl and mix until the sugar is dissolved. Cover and set aside.
  2. Add carrot and zucchini into a pot.
  3. Add 1/4 cup vegetable oil and 1/4 cup of water into the pot. Mix well so that the vegetables are nicely coated with oil.
  4. Spread spinach on top of the vegetables in an even layer.
  5. Drain and cut noodles to desired length and place them on top of the spinach in the pot.
  6. Drizzle sauce on top of the noodles.
  7. Cover and cook for 10 minutes over medium-high heat.
  8. Open lid and stir gently, then gently toss all ingredients with a wooden spoon and tongs for 1-2 minutes until the liquid has evaporated and noodles look cooked and glossy.
  9. Garnish with sesame seeds and serve.
